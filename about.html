About Me




I grew up in Lusaka, the capital city of Zambia. After completing the International Baccalaureate at the International School of Lusaka, I moved to America for college to pursue Computer Engineering with a minor in Mathematics.

Growing up in large family with all of my technology-oriented cousins, I was inspired to go into science. Their interests instigated curiosity in my mind from a young age.I began to read science encyclopedias, and even ordered books about science and technology. When we bought an electrical kit, we were so excited that we spent months making different things. One of my favorite objects in the electrical kit was a motion sensor: At the age of eight, I was amazed that a machine could detect my movements. Consequently, my interests grew and I decided to pursue engineering.

My other interests include travelling and exploring, and experiencing new cultures. Over the years, I've been to several countries including Mauritius, South Africa, UK, India, Dubai, and Kenya. My parents are of Indian origin, and I grew up in a completely different environment in an international school. I became very open-minded, and better at working with people of diverse cultures.

Generally, I love adrenaline. Theme parks and water parks are my favorite places for that reason. I also enjoy trying out new foods, particularly desserts. I would consider myself a chocoholic and an ice-cream addict. That being said, I can eat ice-cream in any weather. My favorite dessert is the classic chocolate fudge brownie with ice cream on the side. Aside from that, my general hobbies include drawing henna, and reading books.
